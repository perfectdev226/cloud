Overview

SEO Studio comes bundled with a service that allows users to circumvent Google's rate limiting of web scrapers. This document explains how the service works, what rights you have when enabling it, and the terms under which you may activate it.

This service is not required to use this script, and is disabled by default. By activating the service, you must fully understand the terms and conditions described below. If you disagree with the terms, please do not activate the service.

Purpose

Some tools in this script perform automated searches on Google Search in order to retrieve essential data. If these automated searches occur too frequently, Google will temporarily block the IP address of your server. This is harmless, except it will break some tools for the duration of the block.

We created this service to fix that problem and keep tools working even when you are temporarily blocked. The next section will explain how it works.

Functionality of the service

To ensure your tools continue working while blocked, this service establishes a network of servers that can perform a Google search on your behalf while you are temporarily blocked. In exchange, you will also make your own server available to occasionally perform searches for the network.

This is similar to using proxies, except you have access to a large collection of fast, high-quality servers at all times, configured automatically for you by the service.

Please note that if we are unable to connect directly to your server over port 80 or 443, we will not allow you to activate the service. Furthermore, if we are unable to connect to your server after the service has been enabled and for an extended period of time, we may disable your access until your server is again reachable.

Eligibility for the service

To be eligible for activation of this service, your application must be hosted on an open website; that is, a website which our servers can connect to in order to perform search queries. If we are unable to connect to your application over the Internet, you will not be able to activate the service.

Impacts on your server

The service will not expose the IP address or domain name of your website or server to other users. Instead, the service will connect to your server on behalf of users, acting as a middle-man to conceal your identity. The service will send no more than twenty requests per hour to your server, and no more than 480 requests in total each day.

The service will regularly ping your server to a) ensure it is online and b) measure latency. During these pings, we may also identify the country in which your server is hosted and record it. This will allow the network to deliver more accurate results, by sending requests to servers near or in the intended country.

Limits of your usage

The service will accept a limited number of requests from your server per day, for which it will execute Google searches on your behalf. The daily limit is not fixed, and may change at any time depending on your overall contributions to and usage of the service. You will be able to see your limit at any time through this admin panel after enabling the service.

If this is your first time enabling the service, you will be granted an initial limit of 100 requests per day. This will automatically be raised to 200 requests per day once we fully connect to your server, and then will be raised to 500 requests per day once we perform our first web search with your server ( this normally happens within 24 hours). After hitting 500 requests per day, the limit may increase or decrease regularly as described in the previous paragraph.

To conserve resources, the service will execute no more than one single request at any given time for your application; any additional requests will be held until the current active request is completed.
